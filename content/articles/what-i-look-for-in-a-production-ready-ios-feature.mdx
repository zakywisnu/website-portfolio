Shipping a feature is not just about whether the happy path works. A production-ready feature should feel reliable for users and predictable for the team that will maintain it.

## User experience first

A feature should answer a few basic questions clearly:

- does the loading state make sense
- does the failure state help the user recover
- are important actions easy to understand

When those basics are missing, even strong engineering work can feel unfinished.

## Engineering readiness

I usually look for a few signals before a feature feels ready:

- the state flow is understandable without reading the whole codebase
- networking and persistence behavior are easy to trace
- important business rules have test coverage
- analytics, logging, or debugging hooks exist where failures would be expensive

This is less about perfection and more about reducing surprises after release.

## Team readability matters

The final check is maintainability. If another engineer opens the feature next month, can they understand the boundaries quickly? Clean naming, smaller files, and deliberate responsibilities often matter more than clever patterns.
